# Solution

## Task 2 — Rolling a Die

1. **Sample space $\Omega_1$ for one roll of the die:**
   $$
   \Omega_1 = \{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6\}
   $$

2. **Sample space $\Omega_2$ for two consecutive rolls:**
   $$
   \Omega_2 = \{(x, y) \mid x, y \in \{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6\}\}
   $$
   It contains pairs like $(1,1), (1,2), \dots, (6,6)$.

3. **Sample space $\Omega_3$ for three consecutive rolls:**
   $$
   \Omega_3 = \{(x, y, z) \mid x, y, z \in \{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6\}\}
   $$

4. **Number of elementary outcomes in each sample space:**
   * one roll: $|\Omega_1| = 6^1 = 6$
   * two rolls: $|\Omega_2| = 6^2 = 36$
   * three rolls: $|\Omega_3| = 6^3 = 216$

5. **Briefly describe what an elementary outcome represents in this experiment:**
   An elementary outcome represents an ordered sequence of numbers that appeared on the top face of the die in consecutive rolls.
